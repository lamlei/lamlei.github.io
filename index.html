Macoin

A city-wide a coin press machine network

Backed by the Macau SAR Government, our coin press machine – MACOIN, has become one of the largest souvenir vending networks in Macau. Since 2018 close to 150,000 coins has been made. The project also has been featured on Macau Daily, Macau Daily Times, Macau Business and various other media

Coin-pressed machine has been a popular tourism product around the world, yet for the longest time it is not available in the SAR. In respond to that, Redirect Limited, a local SME, decided to introduce the concept to the city in 2017. Funded by the government’s Cultural Industry Fund, the project was recognized as a Cultural and Creative program. The same year MACOIN made its first appearance in PATA Travel Mart, hosted by MGTO. The unique experience has proven to be popular among guests.

2017 was also the same year Macau got listed as the “creative city of gastronomy” by UNESCO, in light of this we decided to work with signature local restaurants, and Lei Kei Ice-cream and Tai Lei Loi Kei Pork Chop Bun became our first partners. Before MACOIN these businesses did not have their own souvenirs, the machines and the coins address exactly this problem. Under a profit sharing model, our partners enjoy the benefit of increase visitor counts and revenue, with zero investment cost.

Looking forward MACOIN will continue to work with local businesses and the Macau government, eventually creates a interconnecting network that encourage tourists to discover different parts of Macau.

Sticable

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet consectetur.

Why STICABLE™? Sometimes we really need to unplug, but it is difficult to do when we don't know which is the right plug.

STICABLE™ has a wide range of applications. You should of course put them on your plugs, but why stop there? You can also label your drawer, yes, THAT drawer filled with chargers cables and stuff you just refuse to throw away because "they may come in handy one day". You can also put the stickers on the sockets, on the wall, basically anywhere you want.

We started with creating a set of icons that is universal yet recognizable. The goal is that my 80 year-old-computer-illiterate grandma can look at them and tell me what they are about. They began with tech-related items, such as monitor, router, charger etc., but soon we realized the cable problem exists beyond the tech-savvy crowd, so the icons were expanded to general electronic appliances, such as TV, fridge, and washing machine. At the end we developed over 60 icons, which should most items you can find in a common household.

STEM Toys

an assemly kit with only 7 parts including a small vibrating motor which is generally embedded in cellphones powered by "AA" alkaline batteries. This simple and well-designed kit with a few parts to assemble and no need of soldering is not only a lot of fun for all ages but also a perfect educational kit for students from low-grade and up. We source STEM toys are assemly kits that provide children with learning about making things, electronics, programming, etc., with fun. Nowadays, although children are surrounded by various devices, such as portable game players, smart phones, tablets, PCs, etc., how these devices work is more difficult to imagine than before. Highly-developed technology and downsized parts obscured the mechanism and the principle of such devices so that users have less chance to develop the skill to become a "maker". An American professor forecasts that after 20 years from now half of the jobs we have now will become things of the past. Menial labors are handled by computers and robots, therefore we human being will have to spare a deep thought for what need to be done by humans. In the future, the ability that the society requires for human being is to create highly creative jobs that utilize computers and robots. By assembling STEM toys, children can go through an experience of making radios and robots by combining electronic parts, gears, etc. by their own hands, which will trigger their interest in how things around them work. It is our hope that children grow up with a big dream and ambition and will be able to develop something that are creative, interesting, and that of good service to the society when they are grown up.
